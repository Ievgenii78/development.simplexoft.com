More questions? We've got you covered

Are small loans more expensive than larger loans?

Small loans aren’t as expensive as most people initially think. This is because the APRs advertised usually appear very high. However, this is because they are designed to be borrowed over a short amount of time, giving the lender a small window to make a profit. Many lenders will also provide small personal loans for a set fee, rather than charging interest. If you are unsure how much your loan will cost you, ask your direct lender.

How can I get a small loan quickly?

Small loans can be obtained very quickly. Using Payday UK means the money is normally in your account within minutes. You can apply online with no additional paperwork. We’ve designed our process to make it as user-friendly and speedy as possible. You can apply on any device, including your smartphone. You’ll get an instant decision from our panel of lenders once you’ve completed the easy application process.

Are small loans just for people with bad credit?

No, small loans can offer a great solution to anyone. It’s a way of getting your hands on a little extra cash that will be paid back and finalised, rather than a credit card or overdraft that can tend to hang around for a lot longer. You can borrow with bad credit as well. Payday UK provide access to a variety of loans to suit people with different circumstances.

Is it easy to apply for a little loan?

Yes, the whole process from start to finish is quick and easy. We have a panel of lenders and many of them specialise in certain types of small loans. This means you can get an instant decision, and even those with bad credit have an excellent chance of being approved. We process thousands of applications every day, and we accept at least 95% of them.

Are small loans the same as payday loans?

Most small loans are essentially payday loans. Not all lenders advertise them as such, primarily due to the bad reputation unregulated lenders created in the early days of payday loans. Now all our direct lenders are regulated by the FCA (Financial Conduct Authority) and follow a strict code of ethics and transparency.
